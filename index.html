Finding which dinosaur that was my favorite was actually difficult. I have been in love with dinosaurs since I was a little girl, which led to my obsession with the Jurassic Park Movies (what was incorrect about them, too) and consistent research. I narrowed it down to the dinosaur I do find myself to be the fondest of. This dinosaur is the ANKYLOSAURUS. I was excited to see that the first Jurassic World movie included the Ankylosaurus in their movie. If you remember, this dinosaur fought the Indominus Rex while the two brothers were in the Gyrosphere; unfortunately, the Indominus learned swiftly - like the raptor - and killed the Ankylosaurus by pushing it over to reach it's soft underbelly. The Ankylosaurus is a large herbivore dinosaur that had fused bone body parts to create a harder and stronger exterior, as well as better battle elements against predators. Those consisted of osteoderms (large armor plated like scales), spikes and spines on it's wide back, a large club on the end of it's tail and their skull. Those defense mechanisms (if you will) crushed bones. Though she is a rather slow moving creature, she could move fast in situations that required her to. They were also known to roam in packs, companionship to improve comfort levels. The Ankylosaurus was no taller than 5 feet in height, but she was known as the longest ankylosaurid at the average length of 18 feet. The first discovery of an Ankylosaurus was found in the Hell Creek Formation close to Gilbert Creek, Montana, by the paleontologist Barnum Brown in 1906. Ankylosaurs were around roughly 65-68 myo ago, ending in the Cretaceous period. Triceratops and the T-Rex were two of the other dinosaurs the Akylosaurus lived amongst.
